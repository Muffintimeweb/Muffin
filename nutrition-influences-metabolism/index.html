Nutrition influences your metabolism

Your metabolism is at the heart of your health. If it works optimally, dynamism and longevity are in the appointment. Conversely, it is when it is weakened that the red lights of your health appear , the alterations of your quality of life: catch or loss of weight, fatigue, stress, disturbances of sleep, disorders of the humor , disorders, digestive, repetitive infections, etc.

If these functional disorders are not considered just warning signals to take care of your metabolism, it is then that can appear these so-called so-called diseases of civilization, the prevalence of which has steadily increased in recent decades: obesity , diabetes, cancer, stress, burn-out, digestive disorders, degenerative diseases, autoimmune diseases, degenerative diseases (Parkinson’s, Alzheimer’s, etc.), depression, chronic inflammatory diseases, etc. They all have one thing in common: their risk of appearance is directly related to the effects of the environment on your metabolism.

As such there is a new science, epigenetics (literally what is outside of genetics), characterizing this mechanism. Scientists have until recently considered that genetic information was frozen, like the image of a book. Or not at all. On the contrary, depending on our behavior, we modulate at every moment the expression of tens of thousands of genes that we possess. In short, we write the story of our own book from a detailed plan provided to us by our parents. It’s a bit like having millions of switches on our genes that we choose to switch on or off, based on three key epigenetic factors: diet, physical activity, and management. emotions.

To optimize your metabolism, it is thus to take care of your health in a holistic dimension, ie according to a global and preventive approach. Depending on your predisposition and your lifestyle, an alteration of your metabolism can be expressed differently, but through what I call the 7 nutritional pillars. Let’s see all this in details.

The physical balance

Your physical balance reflects your overall vitality. It is the fruit of the match between your predisposition, your food intake and your specific needs related to your lifestyle. Your body is in a way  the vehicle of your health and it  deserves in this sense all your attention, as much through the signs that it sends you to alert you, than by the way of feeding tens of trillions of cells that the (you) constitutes. Of course, the practice of a physical activity and a peaceful emotional state contribute as much to its balance.

The intestinal pillar

The gut microbiota is now considered the second brain, but its roles on your health go far beyond … We are just beginning to understand the beginnings of its functioning and its actions on your health.

This term of microbiota defines all of the 50 to 100 000 billion bacteria that make up your flora intestina . This last module of many factors including the functioning of your immune system, your risk of diabetes, obesity or chronic inflammatory diseases. It also partly influences the way you digest and your intestinal comfort, it limits the implantation of potentially pathogenic bacteria (generators of diseases), it produces hormones and many molecules regulating your health and your behavior. And probably many other things that we still underestimate …

The intestinal ecosystem , also called an intestinal tripod , defines the balance between your microbiota, your intestinal mucosa (representing more than 300 m2 of surface, more than a tennis court, and subjected to numerous attacks) and your immune system about 70% of which undergoes a ripening phase in contact with the other elements of the tripod.

It is therefore essential to analyze the balance of your intestinal ecosystem with regard to your lifestyle and the quality of your diet, a disturbance can lead to many digestive signs: discomfort, bloating, gas, difficulty digestion or to tolerate certain foods, intestinal pain, etc. Repetitive infections or chronic extra-digestive inflammations can also originate in the disruption of this intestinal ecosystem.

This can come in particular:

  • An imbalance of the microbiota, caused by repeated use of antibiotics or drug treatments, exposure to additives, especially sweeteners, a diet rich in saturated fats and / or sugars, a lack of dietary fiber, an activity intense physics, prolonged stress, etc.
  • A weakening of the intestinal mucosa , qualified intestinal hyperpermeability may be related to the practice of intense physical activity, a high consumption of protein, insufficient chewing, micronutrient deficiency (glutamine, antioxidants, vitamin A, zinc, etc.) or hypersensitivity to gluten.

The intestine is therefore at the heart of your health. It is much more than a vulgar pipe in which food passes. It is the first nutritional pillar, that by which everything starts and on which the food plays a key role.

Glycemic balance

Glycemic balance is a reflection of the effects of your consumption of carbohydrate-containing foods on your metabolism: foods containing starch (pasta, products containing flour, rice, potatoes, bread, legumes, quinoa, etc. .) or sweet taste (sweet products, soft drinks, cookies, fruit, milk, etc.).

The proper regulation of your sugar level (your blood glucose) is crucial for your health. A stable blood sugar level during the day indeed provides many benefits, especially in the morning. Your concentration and your intellectual performances are then optimal, your physical vitality is regular, your satiety is prolonged and the desires of nibbling strongly diminished. And that’s not all. Benefit from a stable long-term glucose also allows you to better control your weight of form, or even lose weight if this is your goal, and especially to maintain a high sensitivity of your cells to insulin, a hormone at the heart of metabolism and therefore your health.

When you eat a food containing sugar, your body will react to stabilize your blood sugar by any means because the brain is very sensitive to changes in blood sugar levels. It is also described as a glucodependent organto the extent that it needs glucose to ensure its functioning (except in cases of total deprivation resulting in the establishment of an alternative energetic pathway, called ketogenic, which is well-known to people performing fasts or high-protein diets for example) . Your pancreas will then secrete this famous insulin, whose role is to reduce your blood sugar to bring it back to a physiological value. So far so good. Yes, except that if you swallow a sugar quickly assimilated, you will secrete a lot of insulin to your pancreas, which will induce a drop in lycemia important and fast, the famous hypoglycemia reaction.

To put it simply, you lack blood sugar because you have consumed too many sugars quickly assimilated by the body. This is how you find yourself tired in the late morning or afternoon, about 3 hours after a meal that has secreted a lot of insulin to your body. You are then irritable, less concentrated and you want to nibble a sweet food, sometimes uncontrollably. When this situation is repeated in the long term, you favor the storage of excess carbohydrates in the form of fat and make your cells increasingly resistant to insulin. They adapt to this situation. Quite simply. Insulin resistance then appears, then diabetes if the situation persists …

Regular physical activity is also one of the best ways to maintain a high sensitivity of your cells to insulin, think about it ;-).

The acid-base balance

The notion of acid-base balance is a little known term and often overused. Indeed, the term “acidosis” is generally used – wrongly – to describe conditions of high tissue acidity. An acidosis is an excess of acidity in the blood. However, your body needs to maintain blood pH in a very narrow range (between 7.36 and 7.42) at the risk of generating risks . He will then use all the means at his disposal to counter the situations that cause an increase in the production of acidic charges.

The main factors modulating the acid-base balance are numerous, starting with the breathing itself. By ensuring all the vital functions, your cells permanently excrete protons (H +) which must then be supported to neutralize the acidity by what we call the buffer systems. Your lungs and kidneys also allow your body to eliminate these acidic charges. However, a lack of physical activity or intensive sports practice, generate additional acid charges or poorly eliminated. Similarly, chronic stress, physical or intellectual hyperactivity can also disrupt the acid-base balance.

Your diet is at the heart of your ability to balance your acid-base balance. Modern dietary habits are such that globally, the vast majority of the population eats too much salt (sodium chloride), especially through processed products alone bringing about 75% of total salt consumption. This includes cold cuts, cheese, bread and industrial dishes. However, this salt is an important vector of acidic charges (via chloride ions). It is the same in case of high consumption of animal protein, especially meat, or sodas. Fortunately, there are in nature very powerful traps of acidic charges. These arefruits and vegetables , very rich in organic salts in the form of citrate. The latter, although responsible for the acidic taste of citrus fruits for example, have in fact a very important alkalizing power.

Thus, a diet rich in salty foods, animal proteins, sodas and industrial products will strongly disrupt your acid-base balance, unlike a food model rich in fruits, vegetables and unprocessed raw products.

This excess of acidity has multiple consequences on the body. In the short term, it causes an increase in the urinary loss of some minerals, especially magnesium, potassium and calcium, the body will draw alkalizing reserves in the bone tissue. If this condition persists, such a loss of calcium increases the risk of demineralization, or even osteoporosis ; The urinary leakage of magnesium causes a sensitivity to stress and chronic fatigue. The risks of tendon or joint disorders, kidney stones or muscle fatigue associated with cramps and body aches can also be a consequence of this state of chronic acidity.

Unfortunately, it is difficult, not to say impossible, to define rigorously the acidifying or alkalizing effect of a food in the body, including urine strips mentioning the pH. The most reliable and practical value in this sense is what is called the Sodium / Potassium ratio and the PRAL ( Potential Renal Acid Load) index . The more positive it is, the more the food has acidic charges, the more negative it is, the more it has alkalizing charges. It is also possible to dose your Sodium / Potassium ratio after collecting your urine for 24 hours (ask your doctor for advice).

Low-grade inflammation

This is a particularly interesting concept. This expression of low-grade or low-noise inflammation , although still unknown, is indeed essential to understand if you want to take care of your health.  It is indeed at the heart of the vast majority of diseases of civilization (cancer, diabetes, obesity, chronic inflammatory diseases, autoimmune or degenerative diseases, cardiovascular risks, etc.) and very closely linked to the quality of food. Recent studies abound in this direction.

As its name suggests, low-grade inflammation is a pernicious inflammation, difficult to identify by conventional biomarkers analyzed when you take a standard blood test, but acting in the long term . It thus favors a ground favorable to the expression of one of these diseases of civilization or simply of the evils of the modern society: the inflammations (all which ends by -it like the tendinites), the stress or the fatigue.

Many nutritional factors may affect this low-grade inflammation:

  • Disturbance of the microbiota or intestinal ecosystem . In particular, a diet rich in saturated fats and / or sugars promotes an impairment of the integrity of the lining of the intestine resulting in increased permeability, causing the production of pro-inflammatory messengers (which the cytokines and LPSs for lipopolysaccharides).
  • Obesity also promotes the production of these pro-inflammatory messengers because of the lesser vascularization of the fat cells .
  • A diet rich in arachidonic acid, a fatty acid from the omega-6 family that is abundant in animal fats (especially pork and beef, and therefore all products containing dairy fat) that promote the production of pro-inflammatory molecules (prostaglandins). . Similarly, an excess of iron in the body increases the pro-inflammatory mechanisms of the oxidative type.
  • A diet low in antioxidant nutrients , especially through a low consumption of plants, spices and aromatics.
  • Repetitive infections, which may themselves be linked to a nutritional imbalance (disturbed microbiota, vitamin D or Zinc deficiency, etc.)
  • The excess of foods with a high glycemic index favors insulin resistance, accentuating the pro-inflammatory mechanisms itself . The lack of physical activity , even if it is not a nutritional factor as such, also promotes low-grade inflammation via this insulin resistance at the muscle level (by increasing ceramide).

When factors accumulate over time or when one of them becomes massive, the system of inflammation control by the body is racing. The cells of the body then go from a state of local inflammation, controlled and punctual, beneficial to health, to a state of systemic inflammation, uncontrolled and perennial, causing cell disruption and disorders. alterations in the quality of life. The cell loses its central property, that of maintaining its ability to respond favorably to the demands of its environment, cellular homeostasis. 

The control of this low-grade inflammation is particularly important: it “sums up” in a way the response of your body to the various factors mentioned throughout your nutritional program.
