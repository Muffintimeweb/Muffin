How to Make Homemade Lasagna

by Yappo · August 5, 2017

The lasagna, generally everyone likes it. The perfect friendly dish par excellence, which is always unanimous at table for young and old. In addition, lasagna is not complicated to do. For a basic recipe, a good Bolognese sauce, a béchamel and lasagna pasta.

I often use ready-to-use lasagna pastes, preferring those that are found in the fresh department and which give better results than the dry versions. But when you have a good hour in front of you, you can also go and make homemade pasta with lasagne!

What’s in the lasagna paste?

It’s not as simple as ingredients: flour, eggs and a little olive oil and salt. We can also replace 1/3 of the flour with fine semolina of durum wheat, but since I never have in my cupboards when making lasagna, I find that it goes very well.

According to the recipes, there are pasta made with a large quantity of eggs. I think that’s enough. With the proportions of my recipe, you can make a large gratin dish for 8 or 9 people, or two dishes of 4 people.

Is it long to do?

I’m not going to hide it, it’s a little longer to do that to open a package of pasta with lasagne. With a robot, the dough is very fast to make, but it must be left to stand for at least 30 minutes before going to the rolling mill. Then, when we have our lasagna leaves, let them dry for at least 30 minutes, which makes a good hour of waiting before mounting his lasagne dish.

Usually, I use these waiting times to prepare my bolognese and my béchamel.

What do you need as material?

The indispensable utensil is the rolling mill. You can find them on the net (at Mathon for example), or in the shops where you are used to buying your culinary equipment ( Geneviève Lethu , TOC , the Cookery of Chartrons for the Bordelais), and it costs between 30 and 50 euros.

Otherwise, if you have a robot like my Kenwood or Kitchenaid, you can add the accessory rolling mill, it costs a little more expensive, in the 70 euros. That’s what I have and it’s super easy when you’re all alone in the kitchen, you do not need someone to turn the crank while the dough is being put through the rolling mill.

What is the recipe?

As I said above, for a large gratin dish, you need 300 g flour (+ or – 30 g), 3 eggs, 1 pinch of salt and a tablespoon of olive oil .

In the bowl of a robot, mix all the ingredients, until a homogeneous paste and not sticky. Depending on the size of the eggs, add a little flour (30 grams on average). Form a ball, enclose it in food film and leave to stand for 30 minutes.

How do you use the rolling mill?

Check the photo below.

First we take a ball of dough in his hand. Neither too small nor too big.

 

We set the ring of the rolling mill on 1, we throw a small spoon of flour on the rollers to avoid that it sticks and one passes there the ball of dough.

A thick strip of dough is obtained that is folded in wallet (thank you the photo)

 

A few pinches of flour on the dough that is passed back into the rolling mill (always on 1), in any direction it does not matter.

All that remains is to pass the dough (without folding it, eh) into the rolling mill by tightening the ring gradually.

That is to say pass it with the ring on 2, then on 3, etc until 6.

Large plates of fresh pasta are then obtained to be dried 30 minutes before using to assemble the dishes of lasagna. To make them dry, the clothes line is very practical …
