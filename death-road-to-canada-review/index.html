Death Road to Canada Review

by Yappo · Published August 7, 2017 · Updated August 7, 2017

If the name Rocketcat Games tells you something, you’ve definitely heard about Death Road to Canada , their zombie roadtrip simulator where you can say “Cool It” to the first person who passes by. If you do not know, let me make you discover one of the most crazy survival that has come out on the market. Tie your belts, we go to Canada!

 

Death Road to Canada is above all a game of zombies: we start with one or two characters generated in a completely random way by the game. It is already the occasion to evoke one of the strong points of the game: The generation of characters, which is frankly to applaud. The characters generated are of a hallucinatory consistency, although sometimes it is a little difficult to differentiate a boy from a girl. But hey, the random generation always has limits, whatever its power.

 

Once the game starts, a small pitch is given: the leader of your fortune group learns that Canada would be a safe place, away from zombies and bandits. He decides to go there, and will have to borrow the Death Road. The bare minimum, of course, but the most important is not the story in Death Road to Canada. Throughout the game, various events will happen. Some will involve zone exploration, either automatic or in the form of a playable mission, where each area must be searched meticulously for food, fuel, ammunition and weapons. These same weapons will be more or less resistant, more or less powerful and more or less manageable. A simple femur will break after a few strokes, but can still be used as a rescue weapon,

TOUGH SITUATIONS

Obviously, you will also face events involving making a choice. For example, some will choose who will watch at night to watch for bandits and zombies, who will explore the city in search of resources, how to deal with bandits … and this is only the classic side of Death Road to Canada, Which will also feature delusional events, which will test your reaction to something totally impropable. Are you going to enter the delirium or will you remain lucid by acting in a classic way? Yeah, well, okay, it’s not very clear says that, but in the game, it’s going to come quickly, no worries.

THE LAW OF THE BEST TRAINED

During your first games, you often see your little team being eaten, losing its hard-won equipment, being killed by bandits, and even knocking on the back without really understanding why you lost. And over time, you’ll understand that every statistic your survivors have has an impact on your entire game. A loyal survivor will endanger his life to protect his neighbor. A good mechanic will know how to maintain the vehicle of the group easily and go much further than the one with no mechanical knowledge. A calm survivor and good shooter will knock down a threatening bear without flinching, where a lambda survivor will panic and be devoured.

A SURVIVOR TO HIS IMAGE

We were talking about character generation just now, right? Well, know that you can also integrate into the game! Friends, family, acquaintances … Is not it great to see “survivor” versions of your entourage being crunched, getting knocked down or fighting violently because the CD player of your car broadcasts a single song ? Besides, speaking of customizable characters, let me tell you that there are also many rare characters capable of turning a whole part upside down. You may well come across Jason Voorhees, Link, fencers, girls from animes and much more, each with his behavior, his powers (useful or dangerous) … Rocketcat has done a very good job on this side -the.

Needless to perpetuate this article, let us hasten to conclude: Death Road to Canada is certainly one of the most wacky and addictive that Steam has known survival. With its effective gameplay, its crazy little delusions, its brilliant references and its soundtrack that delights the ears, this excellent game will tape you for long hours. At least, if you manage to appreciate its particularly retro style which will reject some and its difficulty during your first attempts.

The notes

+Positive aspects
  • Relatively effective gameplay
  • References to each corner
  • This soundtrack that gets into our ears!
  • One can teach a dog to drive!
  • High quality roadtrip
  • Saying “Cool It” to bandits, so rejoicing
-Negative points
  • Clearly not for anti-retro
  • A difficulty that will discourage some
